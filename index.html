FAQ

Why should athletes use music?
Music is one of the few things that has a direct and predictable impact on thoughts, feelings, and effort. As such, it can help regulate energy and nerves, help train and cue focus, increase the intensity and duration of effort, improve the speed of recovery, and lock in learning. Cognitive approaches have a place, but thinking is often inefficient. In other words, change your tune to change your mind!
Who is this supposed to help?
Music support can be offered to individuals, entire teams, or both. If you like music and play sports (or coach/manage those who do), this is for you.
When should I use music in my sport training?
Music is a perfect additive to daily mental game practices, strength and conditioning programs, skill training, pre-game preparation, within game self-regulation strategies, and post-game recovery.
I already listen to music, so why do I need you?
You also already eat, train, and condition. We do for your listening what dietitians, skill coaches, and strength coaches do for your eating, training, and conditioning. Managing your own use of music would be like limiting your nutrition to what you can get from a vending machine. You can do better than that, and the obvious choices aren't always the right ones!
How is this different than a playlist?
A playlist points us in the right direction but isn't specifically designed to help you perform to your potential. We begin where your playlist ends by combining the music you like with your specific performance needs. If you don't know the right song, we find it. And if the right song doesn't exist, we can create it.
Who is behind chordinnate?
chordinnate is primarily the work of Dave Laughlin, who is an applied practitioner of motor learning. That means that Dave help people learn and perform movement-related activities. He also helps athletes and coaches design better practice conditions and helps organizations better support the learning and performance of their athletes. To learn more about Dave's background, head to https://thechordinnation.substack.com/about. For projects that require additional individuals or musical skills, Dave taps into a network of sport and music professionals to assist.
